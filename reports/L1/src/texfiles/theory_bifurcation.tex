\subsection{Bifurcation cascade theory}
A bifurcation refers to a qualitative change in the behavior of a dynamical system as a parameter is varied.
In the context of our system we are interested in how the number of stable orbits reacts to varying one of the two growth parameters, specifically $r_2$ in this lab, while keeping the other parameters constant.
At low $r_2$ values the system has only one stable orbit.
At a certain $r_2$ value, this stable orbit will split in to two undergoing a period doubling bifurcation.
As $r_2$ is increased more, these orbits will move further away from each other and eventually undergo another period doubling.
The difference in $r_2$ between these bifurcations will keep getting smaller with each bifurcation.
After reaching a certain $r_2$ value, the period of the attractive orbits will approach infinity and the system can be said to be chaotic.
