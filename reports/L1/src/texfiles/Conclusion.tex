
In this project, it was studied in depth a two-dimensional discrete-time dynamical system given by two logistic maps. The aim was to investigate how the long-term behaviour of the system changes for different parameter values, using fixed points, periodic points, phase portraits and a bifurcation diagram. Which was completed sucessfully.

A typical progression of nonlinear dynamical systems for this model is shown, which means that for smaller parameter values, trajectories may converge to a single attracting fixed point. The theoretical fixed points were calculated and match the posterior obtained results with the exception of the symmetric case $r_1=r_2=3.1$, where $x=y=1-\frac{1}{3.1}=0.67741935$ exists but due to not being attractive it does not show up in the numerical simulations. This system, therefore, converges to a stable period-2 orbit, from what it's conclude that the existence of a fixed point alone is not enough to describe the observed long-term behaviour. It's then clear that the fixed point's stability is essential. 

Additionally, it was observed that the symmetry between the two populations is broken when increasing $r_2$ while keeping $r_1=3.1$. In this case, the system exhibits a period-2 behavior which then turns into an asymmetric orbit. On the other hand, for larger values of r such as $r_2=3.8$, the bifurcation cascade and the computation of periodic points both indicate a stable period-4 orbit. With even greater values, such as $r_2=3.95$, the system no longer converges to a small number of points, but instead produces a point cloud, suggesting more complex and chaotic dynamics.

Overall, the different numerical approaches give a consistent picture. The numerical calculations, phase portraits and bifurcation diagram all show a transition from simple attracting behaviour to period-doubling and then to increasingly complex dynamics. This leads to the conclusion that deterministic systems can become highly complex, especially when two logistic maps are coupled, generating a behaviour that appears irregular or aperiodic despite being produced by deterministic rules.
