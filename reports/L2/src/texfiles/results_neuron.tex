\subsection{Neuron Automaton Results}


Using random initial conditions in the neuron automation usually led to either the whole system eventually dying out and being inert in the "ready" condition or to infinitely forward moving gliders appearing. By experimenting several initial conditions were constructed that would lead to interesting developments of the system such as a periodic oscillator or a pattern that infinitely generates more moving patterns. Some interesting initial conditions are pictured below. 

First, a number of infinitely moving gliders were identified. Figure \ref{fig:gliders} shows three examples. Those kinds of patterns move infinitely in their initial direction unless they collide with another pattern.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.8\linewidth]{figs/neuron/gliders.png}
    \caption{Three examples of infinitely moving patterns. The two patterns on the left move in the upward direction and the one on the right moves diagonally down and right.}
    \label{fig:gliders}
\end{figure}

By having two simple gliders collide with eachother using the initial conditions shown in Figure \ref{fig:bouncer} it is possible to create two new gliders that move at a right angle to the initial motion giving the impression of two bouncing gliders.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.8\linewidth]{figs/neuron/bouncer.png}
    \caption{Initial condition that leads to two gliders bouncing in a 90 degree angle}
    \label{fig:bouncer}
\end{figure}

It is also possible to set up a pattern that stays in the same location and oscillates infinitely. An oscillator with three distinct steps was identified that is pictured in Figure \ref{fig:oscillator}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.8\linewidth]{figs/neuron/oscillator.png}
    \caption{A three step oscillating pattern}
    \label{fig:oscillator}
\end{figure}

Figure \ref{fig:rake} shows a pattern that moves forward and periodically creates more moving patterns.

\begin{figure}[H]

    \label{fig:rake}
\end{figure}
