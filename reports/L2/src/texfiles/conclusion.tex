In subsection \ref{golres}, we explored Conway's Game of Life and its gliders and guns as the basic building blocks of boolean logic.
As stated in that section this enables Turing completeness and universal computation within the Game of Life.

In subsection \ref{sec:results_nca}, we explored the different emerging patterns from the Neural Cellular Automaton.
This cellular automaton allows for a variety of interesting patterns. Some of these move in a certain direction, some can even produce new moving patterns
and some show periodic behaviour.
An interesting aspect of this is how the most simple glider was just a heterogeneous square of two firing and two resting cells.

In subsection \ref{sec:ghca_results}, we showed that, even when the conditions set out in the lecture for orbits of period $N$ are not fulfilled by the parameters, it is easily possible to construct a simple input that results in periodic behaviour with the period $>1$.
It was interesting to see how the Neumann neighbourhood, in connection with the fact that a single excited cell is enough to excite others, results in diagonally propagating periodic motion where every cell the entire domain cycles through the states.
